Ahmadu Bello Sardauna

1910: Born to the family of Malam Ibrahim Bello

1931: Finished school where he attended Katsina Training College and subsequently became an English Teacher in Sokoto Middle School

1934: Made the District Head of Rabah

1938: Promoted to the position of Divisional Head of Gusau (in present-day Zamfara State) and became a member of the Sultan's council.

1948: Traveled to England on a government scholarship to study Local Government Administration, which broadened his understanding and knowledge of governance.

1952: Won a seat in the Northern House of Assembly, and became a member of the regional executive council as minister of works.

1954: Became the first Premier of Northern Nigeria.

1959: Led his party to win plurality of preliminary seats and the subsequent formation Nigeria's first indigenious federal Government which led to independence from Britain.

1966: Assassinated by Major Chukwuma Kaduna Nzeogwu an Igbo Nigerian Army officer in a coup which toppled Nigeria's post-independence government.
